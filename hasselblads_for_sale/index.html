203FE (+ 110mm f2 FE + E12)

203FE

The 203FE is the best camera Hasselblad ever made. Fully automatic exposure on a 6x6 medium format frame with a focal plane shutter allowing up to 1/2000. It is compatible with all the C/CF/CFi/CFE/F/FE lenses.

The meter is incredibly accurate and will expose in automatic mode at 1/12th of a stop precision, and works in all sorts of difficult lighting conditions. I have exposed in the region of 5,000 frames and it has never made a mistake.

The camera body is a 2002 model, a late production one, and has been serviced twice while I have owned it. The most recent service was mid 2021 and included a full CLA as well as replacement of a couple of parts - this cost me €830. The receipt for this service can be provided.

As you can see from the pictures this would probably be described as in excellent condition - it has some minor cosmetic marks but is mechanically and electrically perfect. Most camera stores, with their overly generous ratings systems, would probably rate this closer to near mint. This one having a recent service and regular use is probably in better condition than those marked "as new" or "unused", as these cameras need regular use to ensure they keep working.

The body comes with a WLF (waist level finder) with a normal (0) diopter, and a Acute Matte D grided screen with split prism - the best screen you can get to ensure spot on focus. An E12 back is also included along with a fresh set of light seals (these are trivial to replace).

202FA (+ 110mm f2 FE + E12)

202FA

The 202FA was the "budget" version of the 200 series bodies. It still features fully automatic exposure on a 6x6 medium format frame with a focal plane shutter allowing up to 1/1000. It is compatible with all the CF/CFi/CFE/F/FE lenses. The difference between this one and the 203FE is that the 202FA doesn't have a manual mode and you can't use the shutter in the lenses, so it is less compatible with existing equipment.

In my opinion it's still just as good as the 203FE, because if you have one of these cameras you don't use it in manual mode - you use it in aperture priority and rely on the 1/12th stop accurate exposures. As with my 203FE this camera has never made a bad exposure. The body is a 1998 model.

I purchased this camera in 2014 as a second body for my main project at the time. The two reasons were to 1) have a backup camera, and 2) have another focal length without having to change lenses. I ended up shooting a significant percentage of the project on this camera. The camera was serviced by Hasselblad in 2014 shortly after I purchased it, they replaced the shutter mechanism in the process. The receipt for this service can be provided.

The body comes with a WLF (waist level finder) with a normal (0) diopter, and a Beattie Intenscreen with split prism - the best screen you can get to ensure spot on focus. An E12 back is also included with a fresh set of light seals (these are trivial to replace), the E12 back was serviced in 2016. Again, the receipt for this service can be provided.

Please note the pictures here include an 80mm lens. I'm selling this with the 110mm lens pictured above, but if you would prefer the 80mm lens contact me and I am willing to drop the price.

907x/CFV 50c II (2020) + XCD 4/45P

907x + CFV II 50c

I probably don't need to tell you much about this one - I purchsed it new in late 2020 so I could use the digital back with the aforementioned camera bodies. I've used it very little, the file counter reads less than 2,000 for the back and less than 500 for the lens.

I've made large prints from this setup, and have made prints from combining it with the 200 series bodies, Xpan lenses, and my large format camera. Its flexibility is what makes it the most compelling if you have existing equipment to use it with. As a standalone camera it's OK. I wrote an extensive blog post about this setup here.

The entire setup is in mint condition with the exception of the buttons on the back - there is a known problem with these buttons that the black covering rubs away as you use them. It's purely cosmetic and Hasselblad will replace them if you want, I just haven't bothered. The sensor of the back is in perfect condition.
